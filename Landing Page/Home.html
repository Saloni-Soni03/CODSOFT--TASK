About Us

Must-Visit Places:

  • Sula Vineyards: Experience the burgeoning wine culture of Nashik with a visit to Sula Vineyards, one of India's leading wineries. Take a tour of the vineyards, indulge in wine tastings, and enjoy scenic views of the surrounding countryside.

  • Pandavleni Caves: Explore the historic Pandavleni Caves, a group of ancient rock-cut caves dating back to the 3rd century BC. Admire the intricate carvings, sculptures, and Buddhist inscriptions while enjoying panoramic views of Nashik from atop the caves.

  • Sita Gufa (Cave): Discover the mythical Sita Gufa, believed to be the site where Sita, the consort of Lord Rama, sought refuge during her exile. Located near the Panchvati area, the cave is surrounded by lush greenery and serene surroundings.

  • Ramkund: Visit the sacred Ramkund, a holy tank on the banks of the Godavari River. It is believed to be the spot where Lord Rama performed his ablutions during his exile. The site holds immense religious significance and attracts pilgrims throughout the year.

  • Trimbakeshwar Temple: Pay homage to Lord Shiva at the ancient Trimbakeshwar Temple, one of the twelve Jyotirlingas in India. Located at the foothills of the Brahmagiri Mountains, the temple is renowned for its architectural grandeur and spiritual significance.

  • Kalaram Temple: Marvel at the architectural splendor of Kalaram Temple, dedicated to Lord Rama. Built in the Panchavati area, the temple's striking black stone architecture and intricate carvings make it a must-visit for devotees and history enthusiasts alike

  • Coin Museum: Immerse yourself in the numismatic history of India at the Coin Museum in Nashik. Discover a fascinating collection of ancient coins, artifacts, and exhibits tracing the evolution of currency in India over the centuries.

  • Gangapur Dam: Escape the hustle and bustle of the city and unwind at Gangapur Dam, a picturesque reservoir located on the Godavari River. Enjoy boating, picnicking, and birdwatching amidst serene natural surroundings.
